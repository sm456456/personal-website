03 : About me

My curiosity turned me into a web developer.

I always thought my true calling is to travel the world. Spending a year in the USA as Au-pair terrified me at first, but I was too curious. My friendly host family made sure I’m experiencing every aspect of American culture and helped me to learned English. After that, I applied for a job as a casino dealer on cruise ships. It seemed like a great idea; being paid while traveling the world. In reality, it was more challenging. Casino dealers (like web developers) have a steep learning curve. When dealing roulette, a dealer must multiply the number of winning chips with either 35, 17 or 8 and add all other side bets on top. All that in a few seconds. Also, one contract meant to work without a day off for 6 months! I loved the experience, but wanted a more technical job.

In Ireland, I learned many internal software programs that I was using for work at Hertz. However, only after moving to Germany, I’ve got something more challenging: to learn German. For 4 months I worked full-time and simultaneously attended an intensive German course every day for 3.5 hours. That’s why I’m very proud to hold a C1 certificate in German language. However, my fascination with computers and software didn’t stop. I started watching one online tutorial after another one. During the first lockdown I participated in EU vs Virus Hackathon. Seeing how developers can bring ideas to life amazed me. That was it, finally there was a job which is a combination of logic, problem-solving and creativity. And the best part; I can never learn everything! I joined WBS coding school full time program. Some days I wondered how one person can remember all that, and other days I felt like a genius.

Today, I enjoy bringing ideas to life in the form of web applications using JavaScript, React JS, Node JS, Express, Mongo DB, Mongoose, GitHub and other programming languages and frameworks. I’m very enthusiastic to deepen my knowledge and to learn more.
